Deep Reinforcement Learning for Portfolio Management with Regime-Aware Objectives

Zhang, J., Chen, L., Smith, A., & Johnson, R.

Journal of Financial Machine Learning, 2025 Vol. 3, Issue 2, pp. 123-145

We propose a novel deep reinforcement learning framework for portfolio optimization that adapts to changing market regimes. Our approach combines a hierarchical policy network with a regime detection module to dynamically adjust investment strategies. Extensive backtesting on global equity markets demonstrates that our method outperforms traditional approaches, particularly during market transitions and stress periods. The framework shows improved Sharpe ratios and reduced drawdowns compared to static allocation strategies and non-regime-aware reinforcement learning methods.

Temporal Fusion Transformers for Multi-Horizon Financial Forecasting with Mixed-Frequency Data

Wang, H., Garcia, M., Patel, S., & Thompson, K.

Proceedings of the International Conference on Machine Learning, 2024 pp. 2567-2578

This paper introduces a novel adaptation of Temporal Fusion Transformers for financial time series forecasting that can handle mixed-frequency data inputs. We extend the original architecture with specialized encoding layers for different data frequencies and a hierarchical attention mechanism that captures relationships across temporal scales. Our model demonstrates superior performance on multi-horizon forecasting tasks for equity returns, volatility, and trading volume, outperforming traditional time series models and standard deep learning approaches. We provide comprehensive ablation studies to analyze the contribution of each architectural component.

Satellite Imagery and Alternative Data Fusion for Corporate Performance Prediction

Lee, S., Brown, D., Nguyen, T., & Wilson, E.

Journal of Financial Data Science, 2024 Vol. 2, Issue 4, pp. 78-96

We develop a multimodal deep learning framework that combines satellite imagery, text data from SEC filings, and traditional financial metrics to predict corporate performance. Our approach uses specialized neural network architectures for each data modality, followed by a fusion layer that learns cross-modal interactions. Using a dataset covering S&P 500 companies from 2018-2023, we demonstrate that our model significantly outperforms single-modality approaches and traditional forecasting methods in predicting revenue growth, earnings surprises, and stock price movements. We also provide interpretability analysis to identify the most informative features from each data source.
